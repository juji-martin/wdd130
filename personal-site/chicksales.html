Rhode Island Red: Renowned for their dual-purpose capabilities, Rhode Island Reds are a farmer's dream. Not only are they prolific layers, often producing large brown eggs consistently throughout the year, but they also boast robust meat qualities. With their hardy constitution and calm temperament, Rhode Island Reds are a breeze to raise, making them an excellent choice for both novice and experienced poultry enthusiasts alike. Their striking mahogany feathers and confident demeanor add charm to any homestead, while their efficient foraging abilities make them a sustainable choice for free-range environments.

Barred Plymouth Rock: A quintessential American breed, Barred Plymouth Rocks are prized for their versatility and docile nature. These chickens are dependable layers, providing a steady supply of large, brown eggs throughout the year. What truly sets them apart is their exceptional meat quality, making them a favorite among backyard farmers and small-scale producers alike. Their distinctive barred plumage adds to their appeal, while their friendly disposition makes them ideal for families with children or those seeking a peaceful addition to their flock. Whether kept in a backyard coop or allowed to roam freely on pasture, Barred Plymouth Rocks thrive in various environments, making them a valuable asset to any poultry operation.

Prices

  • Day-old chicks: Common breeds like Rhode Island Reds or Plymouth Rocks can be found for as low as $2 to $5 per chick, while rare or specialty breeds may cost $5 to $15 or more per chick.
  • Pullets (young hens): Prices for pullets can range from $10 to $30 or more per bird, depending on breed, age, and whether they are already laying eggs.
  • Mature laying hens: Prices for mature laying hens typically range from $15 to $50 per bird, again depending on factors such as breed, age, and egg-laying performance.
  • Meat chickens: Prices for meat chickens (broilers) are often based on weight. At processing age (around 8-12 weeks), live broilers may be sold for $3 to $6 per pound, depending on factors such as breed, size, and market demand.
